SOC Analyst Pathway

Why SOC Analyst?

I am highly interested in becoming a SOC Analyst because the role combines technical investigation, security monitoring, teamwork, and fast decision making during security incidents.

This pathway matches my interest in network security, phishing/email investigation, log analysis, incident response, and continuous threat monitoring.

Role Responsibilities

Monitor Security Alerts

Review alerts from SIEM tools, endpoint systems, network devices, and email security platforms.

Analyze Logs and Traffic

Investigate suspicious activities through logs, packet captures, authentication events, and network behavior.

Respond to Incidents

Follow incident handling workflow, document findings, escalate confirmed threats, and support containment actions.

Relevance to Modern Cybersecurity

SOC Analysts are important because modern organizations face phishing, credential attacks, malware, data leakage, and suspicious network activities. A SOC team helps detect threats early, reduce damage, and protect business operations through continuous monitoring and fast incident response.

How This Interest Connects to My Study

My Computer Network Security background gives me a strong foundation in networking, security concepts, packet analysis, Linux, and system behavior. These areas are important for SOC work because analysts need to understand how attacks appear in network traffic, system logs, email patterns, and user activity.
